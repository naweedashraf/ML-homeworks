Question 1

Given random variable X distributed noramlly($ X\sim N(m,s^2) $) calculate Y = aX + b distribution.

Answer:

We have $ m = E[X] $ and $s^2 = Var(X)$. We have to calculate $ E[Y]$ and $Var(Y)$
$E[Y] = E[aX + b] = E[aX] + b = aE[x] + b = $ am + b
$Var(Y) = Var(aX + b) = Var(aX) = a^2Var(X) = a^2s^2$

so we have: $$Y\sim N(am + b,a^2s^2)$$

Question 4

write a program that produces 20 2D data X=[X1,X2] with independent distributions $ X_{1}\sim N(m_{1},s_{1}^2) $ and $ X_{2}\sim N(m_{2},s_{2}^2) $ draw them in 2 dimensional space.
write 2 dimensional distribution function of X.
$m_{1} = 0.5, m_{2} = 0.5$

$s_{1}^2 = 0.1, s_{2}^2 = 0.1$

$\Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 & ?\\ ? & 0.1 \end{pmatrix}$

Answer:

$\Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} V(x_{1}) & COV(x_{1},x_{2})\\ COV(x_{2},x_{1}) & V(x_{2}) \end{pmatrix}$

$COV(x_{i},x_{j}) = E[(Y_{i} - E[Y_{i}])(Y_{j} - E[Y_{j} ])] = E[Y_{i}Y_{j} ] - E[Y_{i}]E[Y_{j} ]$

in 2D $\Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} s_{1}^2 & s_{12}^2\\ s_{21}^2 & s_{2}^2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} s_{1}^2 & \rho s_{1}s_{2}\\ \rho s_{1}s_{2} & s_{2}^2 \end{pmatrix}$ where $\rho$ is correlation coefficient and equals to $\frac{Cov(x_{1},x_{2})}{\sqrt{V(x_{1})V(x_{2})}} = \frac{s_{12}^2}{s_{1}s_{2}}$
since x1 and x2 are independent then $\rho = 0$ and $\Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 & 0\\ 0 & 0.1 \end{pmatrix} $

finally distribution function of x is: $$ N(X|\mu,\Sigma) = \frac{1}{2\pi|\Sigma|^{1/2}}exp[-\frac{1}{2}(X-\mu)^T\Sigma^{-1}(X-\mu)] $$

$$ where: \mu = \begin{pmatrix} m_{1}\\ m_{2} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.5\\ 0.5 \end{pmatrix} and \Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 & 0\\ 0 & 0.1 \end{pmatrix} $$
